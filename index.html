Who Are The Mandalorians?

The Mandalorians are a proud warrior people whose culture dates back thousands of years. Originating from the planet Mandalore, they have spread across the galaxy, united not by blood but by creed.

Beskar Armor

Mandalorian armor is forged from beskar, one of the strongest metals in the galaxy. It can withstand blaster fire and even lightsaber strikes.

Warrior Culture

Mandalorians are raised as warriors from birth. Combat training is central to their identity, making them some of the most feared fighters in the galaxy.

The Creed

Mandalorians follow a strict code known as "The Way." Traditional Mandalorians never remove their helmets in front of others.

Foundlings

Mandalorians adopt orphans of war, known as foundlings. These children are raised in the Way and become full Mandalorians.

Main Characters

Meet the heroes and warriors who bring this epic space western to life.

The Mandalorian

Din Djarin

Din Djarin is a foundling who was rescued by Mandalorians during the Clone Wars. Raised in the Fighting Corps, he became a bounty hunter and a member of the Children of the Watch. His life changed forever when he was hired to retrieve a mysterious asset-Grogu.

Bounty Hunter Beskar Armor Foundling

The Child

Grogu

Grogu, affectionately known as "The Child" or "Baby Yoda," is a mysterious Force-sensitive being of the same species as Jedi Master Yoda. Despite being 50 years old, he is still an infant by his species' standards. His bond with Din Djarin forms the heart of the series.

Force-Sensitive 50 Years Old Foundling

Heir to Mandalore

Bo-Katan Kryze

Bo-Katan Kryze is a Mandalorian warrior and former member of the terrorist group Death Watch. She seeks to reclaim Mandalore and restore her people to their former glory. Her quest for the Darksaber puts her on a collision course with Din Djarin.

The Series
